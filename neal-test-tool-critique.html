Design Decision

Why I chose concrete over creative

I made a deliberate trade-off when building this tool:

Concrete + actionable + boring
beats
Creative + vague + inspiring

Founders don't execute vague advice. They do execute "text 5 people by Wednesday at 8am."

I optimized for reducing friction over inspiring creativity.

But I Was Wrong
The tool is SO concrete that it's boring. Founders read it and think "yeah, I could do that" but they don't get EXCITED. It doesn't spark new thinking.

The Solution

How to rebuild the tool

Option 1: Use a better LLM with better prompting

Instead of templated if/else logic, use GPT-4 or Claude with a prompt like: "Generate 5 NOVEL, NON-OBVIOUS manual service scenarios. Each must be specific, creative, and something the founder wouldn't think of themselves."

Option 2: Add startup-specific context analysis

Analyze their target market, problem space, and unique constraints. Generate scenarios that leverage their specific situation. Not generic "accountability coach" but "what manual accountability looks like for THIS specific founder type."

Option 3: Hybrid approach

Keep 2-3 concrete, actionable scenarios (the boring but executable ones). Add 2-3 creative, non-obvious scenarios that spark new thinking. Best of both worlds.
